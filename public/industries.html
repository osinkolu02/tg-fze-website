Industries

Who we work with

We don't serve everyone. We focus on two lanes where the margin, storage profile, and execution pain make our operation the obvious answer.

Lane 1 — High margin, high growth

Beauty, Haircare, Skincare & Fragrance

Nigeria's premium beauty market is commercially active and growing. The products are compact, non-perishable relative to food, easier to store than cold-chain goods, and brands can absorb warehousing and handling fees far better than low-margin grocery importers.

Who pays

Mid-sized foreign or diaspora brands. The sweet spot is brands too big to ship ad hoc, too small to build full Nigeria ops. Founder-led premium beauty, hair, and skincare brands testing or scaling in Nigeria.

What they need from us

  • A reliable receiving point near Lagos port
  • Secure storage in a free-zone facility — no customs duty at entry
  • Relabelling to meet NAFDAC requirements
  • Controlled release into Nigeria's customs territory when ready to sell
  • Weekly inventory visibility

What they don't want

  • To set up a Nigerian entity just to hold stock
  • To deal with clearing agents and last-mile chaos
  • To ship small batches at high per-unit cost
  • To lose visibility once product enters Nigeria
Lane 2 — Easy to close, repeat revenue

Packaging, Labels & Specialty Ingredients

Nigerian manufacturers keep getting hit by imported input costs. Raw material imports were ₦1.81 trillion in Q1 2025. There is live demand for staged inventory, smaller releases, and predictable supply for converters, packagers, ingredient suppliers, and brands that don't want to clear everything at once.

Who pays

B2B suppliers selling film, cartons, labels, closures, flavours, baking inputs, sweeteners, additives, and personal-care raw materials to Nigerian manufacturers. Boring customers. Boring is good — boring customers buy again.

What they need from us

  • Staged inventory near their Nigerian buyers — held in the Free Zone without duty at entry
  • Smaller, controlled releases into Nigeria's customs territory as orders come in
  • Predictable supply chain without inland warehousing headaches
  • Stock reporting so they can plan replenishment

What they don't want

  • To ship direct-to-factory and deal with Nigerian customs alone
  • To hold dead stock in a facility they can't see or trust
  • To negotiate with a different clearing agent every quarter
Lane 3 — Second wave

Premium Pantry, Baby & Wellness Imports

This lane can work — food and beverage imports remain large — but the margin and shelf-life profile is less forgiving than beauty, and execution failures punish you faster. We'll serve this category once we've proven receiving, stock visibility, release, and dispatch in the first two lanes.
